## Relationship armor explanation

### Public-facing short version

**Relationship armor is the protection strategy you use when connection feels risky.**

### Expanded version

**Relationship armor is the thing you do to feel safe when relationships feel threatening. It may look like control, shutdown, overexplaining, people-pleasing, criticism, perfectionism, withdrawal, or staying easy to manage. It usually began as protection. But over time, the same strategy that helped you survive can start costing you connection, trust, honesty, and self-respect.**

### Simplest teaching line

**Armor is protection that starts costing connection.**

### When to introduce it

Use “relationship armor” after naming the felt problem first.

Good sequence:

1. “You shut down.”
2. “You overexplain.”
3. “You control.”
4. “You people-please.”
5. “You keep having the same fight.”
6. “I call those protection patterns relationship armor.”

Do not lead with “relationship armor” as if cold leads already know what it means.

## Terminology rules

### Use freely on the website

Use these terms at top of funnel:

- pattern
- protection pattern
- reaction
- same fight
- shutdown
- overexplaining
- control
- people-pleasing
- defensiveness
- withdrawal
- resentment
- repair that does not last
- connection
- trust
- self-respect
- honesty
- different move
- one layer deeper

### Use carefully / define immediately

Use these only after plain-language setup:

- relationship armor
- armor
- relational courage
- family culture
- protection strategy
- survival strategy

How to use:

- “I call this relationship armor…”
- “Relational courage means telling the truth without attacking, collapsing, or disappearing.”
- “Family culture is the normal your relationship keeps practicing.”

### Avoid on cold homepage / lead magnet titles

Avoid leading with these terms:

- operating system
- survival system
- relationship system
- decoder
- map the power
- challenge the operating system
- system underneath the system

These are internal methodology terms or later-stage teaching terms. They can appear inside deeper emails, videos, courses, or methodology pages after the audience has already recognized the lived problem.

### If used later, translate them

Internal term:

**operating system / relationship system**

Public translation:

**the pattern you keep repeating**

Internal term:

**survival system**

Public translation:

**the way you learned to protect yourself**

Internal term:

**decoder**

Public translation:

**guide / map / checklist / quiz**

Internal term:

**map the power**

Public translation:

**notice who manages, who adapts, who shuts down, who carries the emotional risk**

Internal term:

**relational courage**

Public translation:

**telling the truth without attacking, collapsing, or disappearing**

## Final site direction

RobertSawyer.co should be a two-path lead conversion hub:

1. Primary path: individual guide — **Why You React That Way**
2. Secondary path: couples guide — **Why You Keep Having the Same Fight**

The individual path should get slightly more visual weight at first because one person can opt in without partner buy-in. The couples path should remain prominent but framed as a guide for seeing the pattern, not diagnosing the partner.

Public language should be plain, direct, emotionally recognizable, and hopeful. Deeper methodology language should be taught gradually in email, short-form content, YouTube, and paid offers.
