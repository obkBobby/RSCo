For individuals

I know I’m part of the problem, but I don’t know how to stop reacting this way.

You can understand what you should do and still become defensive, controlling, distant, or overwhelmed when pressure rises. The work is learning to recognize the protection underneath the reaction—and make a different move.

  • I get defensive.
  • I shut down.
  • I over-explain.
  • I people-please.
  • I try to control the situation.
  • I spiral.
  • I sabotage closeness.
  • I keep hurting people I love.
  • I know better, but in the moment I become someone else.

For couples

We keep having the same fight, and neither of us knows how to get out of it.

The topic may change, but the underlying pattern stays the same. Each person’s attempt to protect the relationship can trigger the other person’s protection—and the cycle takes over.

  • Same argument, different topic.
  • One pursues while the other withdraws.
  • One manages while the other resists.
  • Someone feels controlled.
  • Someone feels alone.
  • Repair never fully happens.
  • The relationship feels more like a job than a partnership.

The process

How the work moves from insight to change.

1. Identify the pattern

What keeps happening, and what role do you tend to play when pressure rises?

2. Understand the protection

What fear, shame, rejection, chaos, or powerlessness is the reaction trying to prevent?

3. Choose the different move

What can you practice this week that is honest, grounded, and repeatable?
