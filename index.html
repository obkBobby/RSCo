Path 2 — Couple Conflict Loop

We keep having the same fight, and neither of us knows how to get out of it.

The topic may change, but the underlying pattern stays the same. Each person’s attempt to protect the relationship can trigger the other person’s protection—and the cycle takes over.

  • Same argument, different topic.
  • One pursues while the other withdraws.
  • One manages while the other resists.
  • Someone feels controlled.
  • Someone feels alone.
  • Repair never fully happens.
  • The relationship feels more like a job than a partnership.

Path 3 — Attachment Reset

I spiral when dating, texting, distance, or uncertainty activates me.

This path is for the high-functioning person who can feel grounded everywhere else, then becomes anxious, obsessive, avoidant, or self-abandoning when love feels uncertain. It is a focused path, not the default destination for every relationship problem.

  • I overthink texts or silence.
  • I chase unavailable people.
  • I collapse around uncertainty.
  • I confuse intensity with connection.
  • I lose myself trying to keep the relationship.

The mechanism

Relationship armor is protection that starts costing connection.

1. Identify the armor

What do you do to protect yourself when pressure rises: defend, shut down, control, chase, perform, or disappear?

2. Map the cost

Where is that protection now costing honesty, trust, closeness, repair, or self-respect?

3. Practice relational courage

What honest, grounded, repeatable move can you make before the old armor runs the relationship again?
