# RobertSawyer.co Lead Magnet Direction

## Current public-facing rule

Do not lead cold traffic with internal methodology language like “operating system,” “survival system,” “relationship system,” or “decoder.” Lead with the problems people already recognize.

Use plain language first:

- Why do I react that way?
- Why do we keep having the same fight?
- Why do I shut down, overexplain, control, people-please, criticize, or withdraw?
- Why does repair never seem to last?

Then teach deeper concepts like relationship armor, protection patterns, truth one layer deeper, and different moves under pressure through email, short-form content, YouTube, and paid offers.

## Primary individual lead magnet

### Why You React That Way

**Subtitle:** A free guide to the protection patterns that take over when relationships feel unsafe.

**Best audience:** Individuals who recognize that they shut down, overexplain, control, people-please, criticize, withdraw, become defensive, or react in ways they do not like when relationships feel threatening.

**Why it fits:** One person can opt in immediately without needing partner buy-in. It speaks to personal pain first and can nurture toward individual coaching, group coaching, or couples work later.

## Relationship armor explanation

**Short version:** Relationship armor is the protection strategy you use when connection feels risky.

**Simplest teaching line:** Armor is protection that starts costing connection.

Use “relationship armor” only after naming the felt problem first. Good sequence:

1. You shut down.
2. You overexplain.
3. You control.
4. You people-please.
5. You keep having the same fight.
6. I call those protection patterns relationship armor.

## Email nurture path

### Individual guide sequence

1. Deliver the guide and normalize that the reaction is not random.
2. Teach protection patterns and introduce relationship armor.
3. Show what each pattern protects and what it costs.
4. Teach one different move before the next conflict.
5. Invite Start Here, group coaching, or individual coaching.

### Couples guide sequence

1. Deliver the guide and reframe “same fight” as a repeated pattern.
2. Teach common roles without villainizing either partner.
3. Explain how repair fails when the pattern stays unnamed.
4. Teach one conversation that goes one layer deeper.
5. Invite Start Here, RelationSync, or couples coaching.

## Positioning guardrails

- Visual tone: bright, open, clear, and hopeful — “Batman” in truth-telling/message, not dark-background noir as the default website palette.
- Avoid in cold headlines/lead magnet titles: operating system, survival system, relationship system, decoder, map the power, challenge the operating system.
- Use freely: pattern, protection pattern, reaction, same fight, shutdown, overexplaining, control, people-pleasing, defensiveness, withdrawal, resentment, repair that does not last, connection, trust, self-respect, honesty, different move, one layer deeper.
- Tone: direct, serious, clinically grounded, human, hopeful, not shaming.
