Executive summary
This site has a strong point of view. It frames relationship problems as repeating patterns, protection strategies, and armor
rather than simple communication failures. That framing is coherent and distinct. The writing is thoughtful, and the layout is
readable. Nothing about the site feels obviously deceptive or sloppy.
The marketing problem is not message clarity; it is conversion completeness. The Free Guides page exposes disabled form
fields and a visible placeholder for a Kit embed. That is the equivalent of a storefront with the cash register unplugged. On
top of that, the site lacks visible testimonials, credentials, or outcome proof, which means visitors have to trust the idea
before they have enough evidence to do so.
In short: good concept, decent execution, weak proof, broken lead capture, and fragmented funnel architecture.
High-priority findings
•
The Free Guides form flow is not live: email fields and buttons are disabled placeholders.
•
Proof assets are thin to nonexistent on the pages checked.
•
The Courses page is too shallow to function as a true sales page.
•
Conversion links jump to a separate domain, creating brand friction.
•
The site repeats its conceptual language well but leans too hard on abstraction.
What works
•
Strong, non-shaming voice.
•
Clear hero and pathing on the homepage.
•
Clean editorial typography.
•
Good separation between individual and couples pain.
•
No visible JavaScript errors in the pages checked.

Detailed critique by category
Positioning
The position is real and potentially strong: people are not broken; they are protected; repeated conflict is the problem. That is
differentiated and emotionally resonant. The missing piece is sharper audience definition and a clearer not-for-everyone
boundary.
Voice and messaging
The copy feels intelligent, grounded, and humane. It avoids gimmicks. The downside is that it stays at the conceptual level
for a long time. Visitors need one or two grounded examples earlier so they can immediately see themselves in the
framework.
Design and UX
The visual system is competent: decent whitespace, strong heading hierarchy, calm palette, and readable cards. It does not
look broken. But it also does not yet feel premium. Repeated section structures make the page feel formulaic rather than
composed.
Trust and proof
This is the biggest strategic gap. Relationship coaching is a trust-heavy category. Without visible proof, the site is asking for
belief without evidence. That is a hard sell, especially to cold traffic.
Funnel and conversion
The Free Guides page should be the main conversion engine, but the captured browser state shows disabled form controls
and placeholder copy. This needs immediate repair. The rest of the funnel cannot compensate for a broken top-of-funnel
capture path.
Brand architecture
The main brand is Robert Sawyer, but crucial calls to action go to RelationSync pages on another domain. That may be
intentional, but it weakens continuity. The user feels a brand handoff before they have enough trust to tolerate one.
Page-by-page notes
Homepage
Strong hero, clear pain statement, and useful split between individual and couples routes. Needs proof, more concrete
outcomes, and less CTA repetition.
Start Here
Good orientation page, but it feels like a navigation hub rather than a persuasion page. Useful for routing, weak for trust.
Free Guides
Best conceptual page on the site, but the most important problem is that the forms are disabled placeholders. The opt-in path
is not live.
Work With Me
Clear high-level framing and offer stack, but still too abstract. Needs process, outcomes, objections, and proof.
Courses
Too thin to support a purchase decision. It currently reads like a stub.
External RelationSync pages
More forceful and conversion-oriented, but they create a secondary brand layer that may confuse users unless that split is
deliberate and explained.

Marketing playbook assessment
Positioning
•
Good foundation.
•
Needs a sharper definition of the ideal reader.
•
Should clarify what makes this approach different from generic relationship advice.
Proof
•
Weakest major marketing element.
•
Add testimonials, credentialing, case examples, or clear outcome stories.
•
Make trust visible before the user has to click through.
Offer ladder
•
The ladder exists conceptually: free guides -> coaching -> courses.
•
Right now the sequence feels fragmented.
•
Every page should clearly do one job.
Conversion friction
•
Too high for a cold visitor.
•
Broken embeds and external domain jumps increase drop-off.
•
Reduce steps between interest and opt-in.
SEO / metadata
•
Basic metadata exists on the pages checked.
•
Canonical and social metadata were not visible in the checks I performed.
•
Not broken, but not strategically optimized.
Priority fixes
•
Repair the Free Guides embed and test the opt-in flow end to end.
•
Add proof assets across the site, especially above the fold or near conversion points.
•
Rewrite the Courses page so it actually sells the offer.
•
Decide how RelationSync relates to Robert Sawyer and make the brand architecture explicit.
•
Add more concrete examples and outcomes to reduce abstraction.
•
Reduce repeated CTA clusters so the page feels more intentional.
Final verdict: This is a thoughtful brand with a strong point of view, but the lead-gen layer is
unfinished. Fix the capture flow, add proof, and tighten the funnel before calling it
conversion-ready.
